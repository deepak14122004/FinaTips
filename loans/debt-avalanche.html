Method

The debt avalanche method is a debt repayment strategy that focuses on paying off the debts with the highest interest rates first, while making minimum payments on all other debts.

This approach minimizes the total interest paid over time and can help individuals become debt-free more quickly.

Here's an example to illustrate the debt avalanche method: Let's say you have three different debts:

  • Credit Card A: $5,000 with an interest rate of 18%.
  • Personal Loan B: $8,000 with an interest rate of 10%.
  • Student Loan C: $12,000 with an interest rate of 6%.
Using the debt avalanche method, you would prioritize paying off Credit Card A first because it has the highest interest rate.
Here's how you might approach it:

Pay the minimum monthly payment on Personal Loan B and Student Loan C. Allocate any extra money you have for debt repayment toward Credit Card A until it is fully paid off.
After Credit Card A is paid off, you can then focus on Personal Loan B, and finally, Student Loan C.
By following the debt avalanche method, you save money on interest in the long run because you're tackling the highest-interest debt first.
